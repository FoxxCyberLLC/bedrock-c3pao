The CMMC Assessment Process (CAP) v2.0 — which implements 32 CFR Part 170 — defines the full process across **Preliminary Proceedings** and **four phases**. Here's how it flows:

---

## Preliminary Proceedings
Before any assessment work begins, the C3PAO must:
- Receive and confirm the assessment request from the OSC
- Confirm the entity/entities to be assessed
- Frame the assessment scope
- Identify and manage **Conflicts of Interest (COI)** — critical for your platform's advisory vs. certification separation
- Execute a contractual agreement with the OSC

---

## Phase 1 – Conduct the Pre-Assessment
*(governed by 32 CFR §170.17)*

- Review the OSC's **System Security Plan (SSP)**
- Validate the **CMMC Assessment Scope**
- Confirm availability of evidence (Body of Evidence / BoE)
- **Determine readiness** — if the OSC isn't ready, there's an adverse determination process
- Compose the Assessment Team
- Complete and QA-review the **Pre-Assessment Form**
- Upload the Pre-Assessment Form into **CMMC eMASS**

---

## Phase 2 – Assess Conformity to Security Requirements
*(governed by 32 CFR §170.17 and NIST SP 800-171A)*

- Convene an **In-Brief Meeting** with the Affirming Official and OSC POC
- Assess each security requirement using **examine, interview, and test** methods
- Apply **sampling values** for depth and coverage
- Score using the **CMMC Level 2 Scoring Methodology** per 32 CFR §170.24
- Address any **External Service Providers (ESPs)** and their Customer Responsibility Matrices
- Address **Cloud Service Providers (CSPs)**
- Conduct **Quality Assurance reviews** (by someone outside the Assessment Team)
- Hold **Daily Checkpoint Meetings** with the OSC
- Note: Assessors may re-evaluate NOT MET requirements for **10 business days** after active assessment concludes (32 CFR §170.17(c)(2))

---

## Phase 3 – Complete and Report Assessment Results

- Compile results in the required format for eMASS upload (JSON per the CMMC eMASS data standard)
- Lead CCA makes one of three determinations:
  - All requirements **MET** → recommend **Final** Certificate
  - Some NOT MET but a **valid POA&M exists** → recommend **Conditional** Certificate
  - NOT MET and no valid POA&M attainable → recommend **No CMMC Status**
- Conduct QA review of the Final Assessment Report
- Convene **Out-Brief Meeting** with the OSC
- Upload results to **CMMC eMASS**
- Administer **appeals process** if the OSC disputes findings (timeline-bound)

---

## Phase 4 – Issue Certificate and Close Out POA&M

- CMMC eMASS returns the **UID, CMMC Status, and Status Date**
- QA individual generates the **Certificate of CMMC Status** (Final or Conditional) using Cyber AB-approved templates
- Certificate signed by an **Authorized Certifying Official** on file with the Cyber AB
- Certificate delivered to OSC and uploaded to both eMASS and the Cyber AB (`certificates@cyberab.org`)
- If Conditional: OSC may engage a C3PAO (same or different) to **close out the POA&M** within 180 days
  - POA&M closeout follows the same workflow as initial assessment
  - Outcome is either **Final** or **No CMMC Status** — there is no re-issuing of Conditional

---

**Key implication for your C3PAO app:** your workflow needs to track state across all four phases, not just the assessment scoring. Pre-assessment form upload, the 10-day re-evaluation window, the three possible outcome recommendations, and the POA&M closeout as a distinct follow-on workflow are all discrete stages your data model should represent.
